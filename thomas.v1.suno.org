[Verse 1]
[female voice]
[whispering]
Sylvia vanished past the gate,
the second wave still humming,
and Gabi called her “curfew complication”
like that explained the whole thing.
Thomas tried assembling reasons
clean enough to say,
but every answer sounded false
the moment it took shape.

[Pre-Chorus]
Thank you, Gabi.
You’re welcome.
The silence settled strange.
She knew the pauses in his head
before he tried explaining.

[Chorus]
Thomas never lacked for language,
only words that fit the truth.
Every label felt dishonest
once it got too close to use.

And Gabi held his hand like someone
already reading through the act,
while Thomas searched for perfect phrasing
and kept arriving after facts.

[Verse 2]
Billboards, tunnels, rainfall thresholds,
train signals in the dark,
the toll booth girl with beautiful eyes,
the geometry of sparks.

Gabi kissed him in the backseat,
called him Blödsmann, called him fool,
while one half of him stayed present there
and one kept mapping routes.

[Pre-Chorus]
And somewhere in the fractured noise
beneath the city glow,
he realized she was one of few
he never had to perform for.

[Chorus]
Thomas never lacked for feeling,
only structures built to last.
He could diagram affection
yet still miss what had just passed.

And Gabi laughed at every system
he constructed in his mind,
like she understood the man himself
beneath the overdesigned.

[Bridge]
“Marry me,” Thomas thought—
and instantly withdrew it.
Not because he feared the closeness,
but because the word polluted it.

Not possession.
Not performance.
Not some contractual life.
Just Gabi somewhere in his orbit
without girlfriend, fiancée, wife.

[Verse 3]
The hangar lights at Teterboro
cut silver through the rain,
and Thomas asked about her passport
trying not to sound insane.

She reached for reasons not to go:
the studio, the work, Mariain—
and Thomas noticed every answer
carefully avoiding no.

[Pre-Chorus]
“Official plus one?”
Gabi laughed.
The structure somehow held.
Not romance in the usual sense,
but something stranger built as well.

[Chorus]
Thomas never lacked for meaning,
only confidence in names.
The closer something came to truth,
the less conventional it became.

And when Sandra asked "¿Viene?" softly
standing by the jetway blue,
Thomas answered with the simplest word
he fully knew was true.

[Outro]
A second wave.
A black car ride.
New York reduced to lights.
And two dumbasses building something
neither one could classify.
