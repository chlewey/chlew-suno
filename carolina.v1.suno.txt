[Verse 1]
From Aachen down through Köln to Frankfurt Main,
She dragged her bags through terminals alone,
While strangers spoke in accents soft and strange,
And Bogotá kept pulling through the drone.

She helped a tired Ukrainian with forms,
And calmed a restless child beside the pane,
Then heard Colombian voices near the gate
Like distant thunder calling her again.

[Pre-Chorus]
At customs she breathed carefully,
Passport held too tight,
Then saw both parents waiting there
Beyond the airport light.

[Chorus — Variant 1]
“How is father?” Carolina asks
While Nicolás loads the Jeep.
Her mother says, “He hides it well,
But he no longer sleeps.”

“How is father?” Carolina asks.
“He tries to look the same.”
But something underneath the words
Suggests a quieter pain.

[Verse 2]
Beneath the sun on Suba’s winding plain,
The buffet steamed with coffee, fruit, and bread,
While both her families danced through jokes and rain,
And every smile concealed what stayed unsaid.

Her grandpa cursed Bogotá’s strange July,
Her nana blessed her father with the cross,
While tennis talk and Tour de France rolled by
To keep the room from counting newer loss.

[Pre-Chorus]
She moved from chair to chair that day,
Too young for what she heard,
Yet old enough to recognize
The tension past each word.

[Chorus — Variant 2]
“How is father?” Carolina wonders.
An aunt avoids her eyes.
They speak of business trips and summers,
Of fashion shows and flights.

“How is father?” Carolina wonders.
The rumors drift like smoke.
Some say he’s tired, some say dangerous,
Yet no one says it close.

[Verse 1]
From Aachen south through Frankfurt's morning grey,
She dragged her bags and helped two strangers find their train,
Then calmed a nervous man who'd never flown this way,
And heard Colombian voices call her home again.

[Pre-Chorus]
At customs she breathed carefully,
Passport held too tight,
Then saw both parents waiting there
Beyond the airport light.

[Chorus — Variant 1]
How is father? Carolina asks.
The answer comes in halves.
He hides it well, her mother says,
But something in him cracks.

[Verse 2]
Beneath the sun on Suba's winding plain,
Her nana blessed, her grandpa cursed the cold,
While tennis talk kept circling the refrain
Of things the family left deliberately untold.

[Pre-Chorus]
She moved from chair to chair that day,
Too young for what she heard,
Yet old enough to recognize
The tension past each word.

[Chorus — Variant 2]
How is father? Carolina wonders.
An aunt avoids her eyes.
The rumors drift like cigarette smoke
Across a summer sky.

[Verse 3]
She walked her old barrio block by block,
Met school friends over tinto in the afternoon,
And let the city set her like a clock
Before the lunch that came too soon.

[Pre-Chorus]
He ordered for them both with ease,
She stayed on safer ground,
Then Nicolás drove toward the airport
Without making a sound.

[Chorus — Variant 3]
How is father? Carolina wonders.
He laughs, he seems the same.
She lets him think she isn't counting
Every shift and change.

[Bridge]
Backstage in Medellín she saw her —
tall, unhurried, effortless and bright.
The way her father said her name
was different in the light.
Not like the others. Something steadier.
Carolina filed it quietly away,
Then shared a forty-minute flight to Bogotá
and didn't know quite what to say.

[Verse 4]
He wrote the truth in margins, didn't speak,
She pulled her jacket closed against the room,
Then in the car he said what she had tried to seek
And left her standing in the afternoon.

[Pre-Chorus]
In Aachen weeks later he came,
Sophie somewhere nearby.
She used the formal usted still —
not ready to say why.

[Chorus — Variant 4]
How is father? Carolina wonders.
She knows a little more.
The truth arrived in too small a space
And left her at the door.

[Outro]
To forgive is not to forget,
to love is not to know,
she carries what she can of him
wherever she will go.
