[Verse 1]
She came to the Big Apple once
because Thomas was coming,
one suitcase and a borrowed coat—
and why not follow something?

[Verse 2]
The second wave left empty streets,
the cafés closing soon,
a gatekeeper with purple eyes,
and one more lover withdrawn too.

[Pre-Chorus]
But somewhere in those muted nights
past curfew, smoke, and blue,
the girl beside the apartment door
became somebody new.

[Chorus]
Sylvia still a girl then,
still learning her own shape,
through half-lit rooms and winter talks
and mornings waking late.

Not rescued.
Not completed.
Not somebody passing through,
just slowly learning how to stay
inside a life not fully new.

[Verse 3]
One night across the kitchen table,
through coffee, wine, and steam,
they spoke about the kinds of love
that blur with what they mean.

[Verse 4]
And maybe she loved Thomas less
as flesh and bone and man,
than as the proof a different world
might somehow still exist for her.

[Pre-Chorus]
And Gabi’s careful purple eyes
would soften when she spoke,
with just enough exposed beneath
the irony and smoke.

[Chorus]
Sylvia still a girl then,
still learning how to feel,
through conversations after dark
that somehow sounded real.

Not waiting
to be chosen,
not orbiting on cue,
just finding parts of who she was
reflected back in someone new.

[Bridge]
And then she understood at last:
Thomas had never been
a destination or a prize
to somehow finally win.

He was the road that brought her there,
the motion and the spark,
but she herself had crossed the bridge
that winter through the dark.

[Verse 5]
New York landmarks through second wave:
the skyline, bridge, and train,
the grocery on Atlantic Ave,
the silence after rain.

[Pre-Chorus]
On December twenty-third
she packed without regret,
and left behind the girl who came
not knowing herself yet.

[Chorus]
Sylvia not just a girl now,
not drifting place to place,
but someone who could meet the world
without erasing space.

Not perfect.
Not finished.
Still uncertain what was true,
but carrying home a quieter kind
of personhood she grew into.

[Outro]
A year of learning.
A month of learning.
The city washed in blue.
And Sylvia smiling in the glass
like somebody she knew.
