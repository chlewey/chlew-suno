[Verse 1]
Her father built his towers tall
on Casablanca sand,
and watched his daughter learn the world
as only daughters can.
She sat beside the men who talked,
outside the door they drew,
and memorized the way that power
moves before it moves.
[Verse 2]
She studied business first in Paris,
London gave her more,
then Cordon Bleu for only her —
that's what the kitchens were.
Not for the firm, not for the name,
not what a father needs,
just terracotta at her lips
and salt, and what she pleased.
[Pre-chorus]
But somewhere in the moving
she began to see the shape —
the choosing not to settle
is its own kind of escape.
[Guitar riff]
[Chorus]
She carries her own gravity,
no orbit holds her long.
She knows the cost of staying still
and keeps on moving on.
[Verse 3]
In Ibiza she saw him working
every corner of the room,
and let him think they'd never met —
she'd waited three years for the bloom.
He checked her Instagram by morning,
found out what she'd always known.
The asymmetry amused her.
She had never been his to own.
[Pre-chorus]
But somewhere in the laughing
she began to feel the weight —
depth might not require stopping.
She hasn't solved it. Yet. Too late?
[Guitar riff]
[Chorus]
She carries her own gravity,
no orbit holds her long.
She knows the cost of staying still
and keeps on moving on.
[Verse 4]
She moves through Paris, London, Tunis,
through empty legs and borrowed nights,
through airport hotels with Susana,
through all the odd and decent lights.
Lucía she watches like a lesson —
a woman who kept her own ground.
Diana's thirty-three and noticing
the thing she's circling around.
[Bridge]
She is not opposed to depth.
She is opposed to what depth costs —
the stillness, the expected shape,
the version of herself she'd lose.
But the if is doing heavy lifting now,
and she can feel it in the room
the way she felt him in Casablanca,
years before he knew her name.
