[Verse 1]
New York beneath the second wave,
the avenues half-lit,
Gabi watched the airport codes
like promises unfit.

[Verse 2]
The elevator softly chimed,
the doorframe filled with light,
and something teal was next to him
she hadn’t planned that night.

[Pre-Chorus]
So she reached first for practicals,
for boundaries and tone,
the kind of calm that lets you hide
how quickly you were thrown.

[Chorus]
No titles.
No promises.
Still, somehow, she stayed near.
Like being chosen carefully
without the usual words to hear.

[Verse 3]
Brooklyn wrapped in radiator heat,
shared blankets, coffee steam,
a girl with teal-dyed hair who asked
too much with quiet green.

[Pre-Chorus]
And somewhere in those winter nights
the seams gave way under heat,
through casual hands and honest talk
and truths that nearly slipped.

[Chorus]
No titles.
No guarantees.
No neat romantic frame.
Just Thomas reaching out to her
without pretending they were named.

[Bridge]
“Girlfriend” sounded wrong somehow.
“Wife” was even worse.
So they built softer language there
between affection, joke, and curse.

Official plus one.
She laughed too hard.
Which probably meant yes.

[Verse 4]
The runway lights at Teterboro
cut gold across the rain,
and Gabi climbed the aircraft stairs
without asking what they became.

[Pre-Chorus]
The world outside was shutting down,
but somewhere in between
she found a shape of closeness still
that lived outside routine.

[Chorus]
No titles.
No promises.
Still, somehow, it was real.
A place beside somebody else
without surrendering the wheel.

[Outro]
Second wave.
Cabin lights.
The engines humming low.
And Gabi letting herself stay
where she once planned to go.
